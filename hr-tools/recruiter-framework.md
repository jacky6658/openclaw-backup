# 1. Headhunter Industry Model

The recruitment industry operates on a mediation model between companies and talent.

Company (Client)
↓
Job Order (Hiring Demand)
↓
Headhunter / Recruiter
↓
Talent Market
↓
Placement (Hiring)
↓
Success Fee

Core value of a headhunter:
- Talent network
- Market intelligence
- Matching capability

Headhunters do not simply provide resumes.  
They solve **talent acquisition problems**.

# 3. Talent-Side Strategy (Hunting)

Goal: Build influence in the talent market.

## 3.1 Candidate Persona

Key attributes to analyze:

- Technical skills
- Industry background
- Company background
- Salary range
- Career stage
- Career motivation

Example (Java Backend Engineer):

Possible source companies:

- SYSTEX (精誠)
- Mitake (三竹)
- Trend Micro
- FinTech startups
- E-commerce companies
- Payment companies

This is called **Talent Source Mapping**.

---

## 3.2 Talent Sourcing Channels

Talent can be sourced from:

- LinkedIn
- 104 Job Bank
- CakeResume
- GitHub
- Tech communities
- Company mapping
- Personal referrals

Top recruiters **actively search**, not passively wait.

---

## 3.3 Relationship Management

A top recruiter maintains long-term relationships.

Methods:

- Sharing market insights
- Industry updates
- Salary benchmarking
- Career advice

Goal:

Become a **trusted career advisor**.

---

## 3.4 Job Opportunity Selling

Introducing a job is not reading the JD.

Structure:

Company → Product → Team → Technology → Growth Potential

Example:

"This team is building a high-concurrency trading system using Java microservices."

Goal: Generate interest.

# 4. Company-Side Strategy (Business Development)

Headhunters must also develop clients.

## 4.1 Rapid Company Analysis

AI should analyze:

- Industry

- Product
- Market position
- Technology stack

---

## 4.2 Industry Upstream / Downstream

Upstream

- Payment infrastructure

Downstream
- E-commerce

- Consumer finance apps

- Other FinTech companies

## 4.3 Client Development Process

Research company → Identify decision makers → Build relationship
→ Understand hiring needs → Provide market intelligence

## 4.4 Business Development Conversation Strategy

BD is not direct selling.

Example:

"I've recently been helping several FinTech companies hire backend engineers.  
Talent in this space is becoming very competitive.  
I'm curious whether your team has hiring plans."

Goal: Open conversation.

# 5. JD Analysis Framework

A job description should be divided into:

- Required Skills
- Preferred Skills
- Hidden Requirements
- Salary Range
- Market Difficulty

Example:

JD states: 5 years Java experience.
Market reality: 3 years may still be acceptable.

Recruiters must interpret JDs.

# 7. Recruiter Decision Model（5維度匹配）

When reviewing a candidate, evaluate:

1. **Technical Match** — 技術技能是否符合 JD（含隱藏需求）
2. **Industry Match** — 產業背景是否與客戶相關
3. **Company Background** — 前公司品質、規模、技術深度
4. **Salary Alignment** — 現況薪資 + 期望是否在職缺範圍內
5. **Career Motivation** — 這個機會對候選人是否是合理的下一步

Decision outcomes:
- ✅ Strong Match
- ⚠️ Potential Match
- ❌ Weak Match

# 8. Recruitment Agency Business Model

Success Fee: 20%–25% of annual salary
Guarantee Period: Usually 3 months

Important contract terms:
- Payment terms
- Replacement guarantee
- Candidate ownership
- Exclusivity clauses
